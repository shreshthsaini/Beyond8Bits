Abstract

High Dynamic Range (HDR) videos deliver richer luminance, wider color gamut, and deeper contrast than Standard Dynamic Range (SDR) content. The explosive rise of User-Generated Content (UGC) on platforms such as YouTube, Instagram and TikTok has, however, introduced a long tail of capture, editing and compression distortions that are specific to HDR and poorly characterized by existing quality models — most of which were tuned on professionally generated or SDR content. We introduce Beyond8Bits, the first large-scale, crowd-sourced psychometric study of HDR-UGC video quality. The dataset brings together 5,917 source videos (real, in-the-wild user captures plus a curated Vimeo crowd), transcoded across multiple resolutions and bitrates to yield 41,419 distorted videos, on which we collected ~1.46 million perceptual opinion scores via Amazon Mechanical Turk. Building on this resource, we propose a novel reasoning-aware MLLM baseline trained with HAPO (HDR-Aware Policy Optimization), a reinforcement-learning objective that explicitly aligns multi-modal LLM reasoning with HDR-specific perceptual cues. Beyond8Bits subsumes our earlier CHUG (ICIP '25) and BrightVQ (WACV '26 Oral) releases and is, to the best of our knowledge, the largest and most diverse publicly available HDR-UGC VQA resource to date.

What's in Beyond8Bits

Beyond8Bits unifies two prior subjective studies (CHUG, BrightVQ) and adds a substantially larger Vimeo-sourced HDR-UGC partition, plus a new reasoning-aware MLLM baseline. Each source video is transcoded across a multi-rung bit-ladder to expose realistic compression, up/down-scaling and re-encoding distortions.

Largest HDR-UGC study

~1.46 M opinion scores across 41K+ videos — an order of magnitude beyond prior HDR VQA datasets.

Two complementary sources

In-the-wild phone captures (Crowd) and curated professional-ish Vimeo HDR content, both transcoded to a common bit-ladder.

Fine-grained distortions

Multiple resolutions (360p → 1080p) and bitrates capture realistic adaptive-streaming artifacts alongside capture-side HDR pitfalls.

Reasoning-aware baseline

A multimodal LLM trained with our HAPO RL objective produces interpretable, HDR-grounded quality judgments.

Rich metadata

Per-video MOS / SOS / raw ratings, resolution, bitrate, framerate, orientation, content group and train/test split.

Open & reusable

All metadata and the publish-ready subsets are released under a permissive license for non-commercial research.

Dataset Composition

Beyond8Bits is partitioned into a Crowd split (smartphone-captured HDR-UGC from a curated crowd study) and a Vimeo split (permissively-licensed Vimeo HDR uploads). Both partitions use the same transcoding grid, rating instrument and subject pool, enabling apples-to-apples cross-domain analysis.

Subjective Study Interface

Ratings were collected on Amazon Mechanical Turk with a continuous single-stimulus rating bar, HDR-capable display attestation, and gold / self-consistency screening HITs interleaved throughout the session.

The web-based AMT rating interface used across the CHUG, BrightVQ and Vimeo sub-studies that together form Beyond8Bits.

How to Access the Dataset

We release per-video metadata (IDs, MOS, SOS, resolution, bitrate, orientation, framerate, content group, split) and the corresponding video payloads via an S3-hosted mirror. The CHUG publish-ready subset is fully available now; remaining partitions are being staged — check the GitHub for the current manifest.

Clone the GitHub repository

All metadata files, ID lists, rating CSVs and licensing info live in the Beyond8Bits GitHub repository. The paper supplementary contains detailed per-field documentation.

git clone https://github.com/shreshthsaini/Beyond8Bits.git
cd Beyond8Bits

Grab the video-ID manifest

The published manifest for the CHUG-compatible crowd subset is at data/Beyond8Bits_publish_crowd.txt (one hashed ID per line) with matched MOS / SOS in data/Beyond8Bits_publish_crowd.csv. Full-dataset manifests (Beyond8Bits_full.txt) will be staged in the same folder.

Download a single video with the AWS CLI

Replace VIDEO_ID with any hashed ID from the manifest:

aws s3 cp s3://ugchdrmturk/videos/VIDEO_ID.mp4 ./Beyond8Bits_Videos/

Bulk download all videos

To mirror the full published partition in one shot:

cat data/Beyond8Bits_publish_crowd.txt | while read video; do
  aws s3 cp s3://ugchdrmturk/videos/${video}.mp4 ./Beyond8Bits_Videos/
done

Stream a video directly in the browser

Replace VIDEO_ID below to play any video without the AWS CLI:

https://ugchdrmturk.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/videos/VIDEO_ID.mp4

Example: 9ae245a27cc5ea9d2f3fae9692250281.mp4

Part of a Family of HDR-UGC Releases

Beyond8Bits consolidates and extends two earlier releases from our lab. If you use Beyond8Bits, please also consider citing its predecessors.

ICIP 2025 · Dataset

CHUG

Crowdsourced HDR-UGC Quality Dataset — 856 sources, 5,992 videos, 211,848 ratings. The crowd-captured seed study that Beyond8Bits extends.

 WACV 2026 Oral · Dataset + Method

BrightRate / BrightVQ

HDR-UGC VQA dataset (300 src / 2,100 videos / 73,794 ratings) and a classical feature-based quality model. Subset of the Beyond8Bits corpus.

LIVE HDR UGC Program

Long-running program at LIVE on HDR capture, tone-mapping, inverse tone-mapping, and perceptual quality assessment.
